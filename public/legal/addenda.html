ReCater — University-specific Terms Addenda

Last updated: 7 June 2026 Effective: 1 July 2026 Version: 2.0 Template (to be completed per University Tenant)

What this is. Each University Tenant may have additional state-specific, FERPA-specific, or AUP-specific terms that need to be acknowledged by Users. Free Food Alert maintains three such addenda (Portland Community College, Oregon State University, University of North Carolina Wilmington); ReCater uses a similar template, expanded to cover FERPA explicitly and to support both email-auth and SSO authentication paths. The University fills in the bracketed fields before publishing; ReCater hosts a signed copy on getrecater.com/legal/addenda/.html.

This is a template, not a contract. The bracketed fields (e.g., [UNIVERSITY NAME], [STATE], [EFFECTIVE DATE]) MUST be completed by the University and ReCater for the executed version. The filled-in example in section C uses the placeholder name “Pinecrest University” and is purely illustrative; it is not a commitment by ReCater or any University, and it has no legal effect.

Questions. Email legal@getrecater.com.

A. Template Instructions

The University Tenant completes the following bracketed fields and publishes the completed addendum at getrecater.com/legal/addenda/<university-slug>.html. The addendum becomes part of the binding User Terms for Users within the Tenant.

Field Where it goes Notes
[UNIVERSITY NAME] Throughout The legal name of the University
[UNIVERSITY SHORT NAME] Throughout The brand name (e.g., “ACME University”)
[STATE] §1, §3 State where the University is located
[EFFECTIVE DATE] Header The date the addendum becomes effective
[AUP URL] §2 Link to the University’s Acceptable Use Policy, if one exists
[SSO URL] §4 If SSO is configured, link to the University’s IdP entry point
[CAMPUS HEALTH CONTACT] §6 Email or phone for campus health services
[SPECIAL PROVISIONS] §8 Any additional university-specific provisions (e.g., age exception, dietary policy)
[VENUE] §3 City/county/state for judicial venue, if the default Wilmington, Delaware is not used
[LIABILITY CAP TERMS] §7 If liability cap is changed by mutual agreement; otherwise leave blank and the default User Terms §4.2 applies

B. The Template (University fills in the bracketed fields)

B.1 Identifying information

ReCater University Addendum — [UNIVERSITY NAME]

Effective: [EFFECTIVE DATE] State of governing law: [STATE] Authentication path: [email-auth OR SSO — circle one]

These ReCater University Addenda — [UNIVERSITY NAME] (the “Addendum”) supplement and are incorporated by reference into the ReCater User Terms of Use (the “User Terms”) and the ReCater Privacy Policy (the “Privacy Policy”). These Addenda apply to all Users affiliated with [UNIVERSITY NAME] through a verified campus email at the University Tenant’s approved email domain, or through the University’s Identity Provider.

B.2 Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Use of ReCater at [UNIVERSITY NAME] is governed by the University’s Acceptable Use Policy at [AUP URL]. The AUP governs behavior on the University’s network and University systems. ReCater defers to the AUP where the AUP imposes additional requirements on Users; ReCater’s User Terms and Privacy Policy govern where ReCater’s requirements are additional or more specific.

B.3 Governing law and venue

This Addendum, and any dispute arising out of or related to the use of the ReCater platform within the [UNIVERSITY NAME] Tenant, is governed by the laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any judicial proceedings permitted under the dispute-resolution provisions of the User Terms §11 shall be brought in the state or federal courts located in [VENUE] (or, if left blank, in the default venue in the User Terms §12), and the User and ReCater each consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of such courts.

B.4 SSO and authentication

If [UNIVERSITY NAME] has configured Single Sign-On (SSO) via [SSO URL], Users affiliated with the University will be authenticated through the University’s Identity Provider. The opaque identifier from the IdP is what ReCater stores. ReCater does not receive or store the User’s real name, university ID number, or academic record through the SSO integration. If [UNIVERSITY NAME] has configured email-auth (the default), Users will sign up with their approved @[UNIVERSITY DOMAIN] email address. The email is stored in ReCater’s authentication layer (Supabase Auth) and is not displayed in the app or in standard reports. (See the User Terms §10.2 for the limited, audited circumstances in which authentication-layer identifiers may be accessed by Tenant administrators or ReCater platform administrators.)

B.5 FERPA disclosure

Under federal law, the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), 20 U.S.C. § 1232g, and its implementing regulations at 34 C.F.R. Part 99, students of [UNIVERSITY NAME] have certain rights regarding their education records. ReCater is designed to minimize data collection such that the platform does not require access to student education records, rosters, transcripts, course enrollment, or financial aid data. In-app identity is pseudonymous; authentication-layer data is stored separately. Whether ReCater falls within or outside the University’s specific FERPA obligations depends on the deployment configuration and the University’s own legal interpretation. The University should consult its own counsel.

By using ReCater within the [UNIVERSITY NAME] Tenant, the User confirms that the User has read this FERPA disclosure and understands that the User’s in-app identity is pseudonymous, that the User’s authentication-layer identifier is not displayed in the app, and that ReCater does not produce identifiable student-level exports without explicit written authorization from the University.

B.6 Food safety escalation

Food safety incidents at [UNIVERSITY NAME] should be reported through the ReCater app’s “Report a User” → “Food safety” tag, AND directly to [CAMPUS HEALTH CONTACT]. The University Tenant may impose additional food safety or vendor requirements beyond the ReCater Food Safety Statement; if so, those requirements are added to the addendum and govern for Users within the Tenant.

B.7 University-specific liability cap

The liability cap in the User Terms §4.2 controls by default. The University and ReCater may, by mutual written agreement, agree to a different cap for claims arising from the ReCater platform within the [UNIVERSITY NAME] Tenant. Any such agreement must be entered in [LIABILITY CAP TERMS] and signed by both parties. The cap may not be increased above what ReCater’s then-current insurance program supports, and the University should not assume any specific cap is in effect unless and until this section is completed and signed.

B.8 Special provisions

[UNIVERSITY NAME] has the following special provisions, if any, that apply to its Tenant:

  • Eligibility exceptions (if any): [e.g., “Users aged 16 and older who are affiliated with [UNIVERSITY NAME] as student organization members are eligible to use the platform, notwithstanding the 18+ requirement in the User Terms §3.1.” OR “All users affiliated with [UNIVERSITY NAME] are eligible to use the platform, including users under 18, in accordance with applicable state law.”]
  • University-specific dietary or allergen requirements (if any): [e.g., “All food posts must include allergen information for the top 9 FDA allergens. Posts missing allergen information will be auto-hid by the platform.”]
  • University-specific posting restrictions (if any): [e.g., “Only faculty, staff, and registered student organizations are eligible to post. Personal student accounts cannot post.”]
  • Other (if any): [Free-text field for any other special provisions]

B.9 Acceptance

By using ReCater within the [UNIVERSITY NAME] Tenant, the User confirms that the User has read and agrees to the ReCater User Terms of Use, the ReCater Privacy Policy, the ReCater Food Safety Statement, the ReCater Data Processing Addendum, and this [UNIVERSITY NAME] Addendum.

D. Per-State Addenda (Optional, Auto-Included)

These per-state addenda are included by default in every university addendum unless the university opts out in writing. The current per-state addenda are drafts based on ReCater’s review of each state’s law; they are not legal opinions and the University should consult its own counsel.

D.1 California

If the University is located in California, the following applies:

  • CCPA / CPRA rights: California residents have the right to know, delete, correct, opt out of sale, and limit the use of sensitive personal information, as described in the Privacy Policy §11.8.
  • Shine the Light: California Civil Code § 1798.83 permits California residents to request information about the categories of personal information disclosed to third parties for those third parties’ direct-marketing purposes. ReCater does not disclose personal information to third parties for their direct-marketing purposes.
  • Food-safety disclaimer: nothing in the Food Safety Statement or the User Terms waives rights that cannot be waived under California law.

D.2 Massachusetts

If the University is located in Massachusetts, the following applies:

  • 201 CMR 17.00: ReCater’s security program is designed to align with the Massachusetts Standards for the Protection of Personal Information of Residents of the Commonwealth (201 CMR 17.00). A written information security program (WISP) is maintained for ReCater’s internal operations and is available on request. ReCater does not represent that the WISP satisfies any particular University’s independent obligations under 201 CMR 17.00.
  • Data breach notification (M.G.L. c. 93H): ReCater will notify the University and affected Users in accordance with Massachusetts data breach notification law, including the requirement to notify the Attorney General and the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation.

D.3 Texas

If the University is located in Texas, the following applies:

  • Texas Good Samaritan Food Donation Act: the Texas Good Samaritan Food Donation Act (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 76.001) provides civil and criminal liability protection for good-faith food donations. ReCater’s role is not a “donation” within the meaning of the Act; the Food Safety Statement §2 governs the assumption of risk.
  • Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (effective July 1, 2024): Texas residents have the right to access, correct, delete, and obtain a portable copy of their personal data, and to opt out of targeted advertising. ReCater does not engage in targeted advertising. ReCater’s compliance with TDPSA is described in the Privacy Policy §11.8.

D.4 New York

If the University is located in New York, the following applies:

  • NY SHIELD Act: ReCater’s security program is designed to align with the New York Stop Hacks and Improve Electronic Data Security (SHIELD) Act. ReCater maintains a written information security program for its internal operations and is available on request.
  • NY Food Donation and Food Scraps Recycling Law: nothing in the Food Safety Statement or the User Terms is intended to override the rights of any party under the New York Food Donation and Food Scraps Recycling Law.

D.5 Washington (state)

If the University is located in Washington, the following applies:

  • My Health My Data Act (effective March 31, 2024): ReCater does not collect consumer health data within the meaning of the Washington My Health My Data Act. The Platform’s dietary flags are not “consumer health data” because they are organizer-entered disclosures about the food, not user-specific health information.
  • Washington Good Food Act: nothing in the Food Safety Statement or the User Terms is intended to override the rights of any party under the Washington Good Food Act.

D.6 Other states

For states not listed above, ReCater will work with the University to add a per-state addendum on request. Contact legal@getrecater.com.

E. Acceptance Record

ReCater will maintain a record of which University Tenants have completed and published addenda under this template, with effective dates and version numbers. The record is available on request to legal@getrecater.com.

End of University-specific Terms Addenda (Version 2.0).

This template is informed by Free Food Alert’s per-university addenda for Portland Community College, Oregon State University, and University of North Carolina Wilmington, which are public and served as a benchmark. It is expanded to cover FERPA explicitly, SSO authentication, and the per-state variations that ReCater anticipates across U.S. higher-education markets.
