ReCater — Data Processing Addendum

Last updated: 7 June 2026 Effective: 1 July 2026 Version: 2.0 ReCater Inc. and the University Tenant

This Data Processing Addendum (the “DPA”) is entered into between ReCater Inc. (“ReCater,” “Processor”) and the University Tenant identified in the executed Master Services Agreement (the “University,” “Controller”). It forms part of the Master Services Agreement and applies to the extent that ReCater processes Personal Data on behalf of the University in connection with the ReCater platform.

Plain-English summary. When your university uses ReCater, your university is the “controller” of the data (it’s the university that decides why and how the data is used), and ReCater is the “processor” (we process the data on the university’s behalf, under the university’s instructions). This DPA spells out exactly what data ReCater processes, who else is involved (the sub-processors), what security measures we take, what happens if something goes wrong, and what the university can do to verify our compliance. It also covers international data transfers (because ReCater is US-based) and what happens to the data if the university stops using ReCater.

Questions about this DPA. Email legal@getrecater.com.

1. Definitions

In this DPA, the following terms have the meanings given below. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meaning given in the Master Services Agreement or the Privacy Policy.

  • “Personal Data” has the meaning given in the GDPR (Article 4(1)) and includes any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that ReCater processes on behalf of the University.
  • “Processing” has the meaning given in the GDPR (Article 4(2)) and includes any operation performed on Personal Data.
  • “Controller” means the University, which determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data.
  • “Processor” means ReCater, which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
  • “Sub-processor” means any third party engaged by ReCater to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
  • “Data Subject” means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates, including students, organizers, and dashboard admins.
  • “Personal Data Breach” means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.
  • “Supervisory Authority” means an independent public authority established by a Member State pursuant to the GDPR (Article 51) or its equivalent in the UK, Switzerland, or another relevant jurisdiction.
  • “GDPR” means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

2.1 The University (Controller)

The University is the Controller. As the Controller, the University:

  • Determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data within its Tenant.
  • Determines the legal basis for the Processing under the GDPR or other applicable data-protection law.
  • Is responsible for providing notice to Data Subjects (students, organizers, dashboard admins) about the Processing, including through the University’s own privacy notice.
  • Is responsible for responding to Data Subject requests, with ReCater’s reasonable assistance as described in §9.
  • Is responsible for ensuring that the University has the legal authority to share any Personal Data it provides to ReCater (e.g., authentication-layer email, IdP assertions, configuration data).

2.2 ReCater (Processor)

ReCater is the Processor. As the Processor, ReCater:

  • Processes Personal Data only on documented instructions from the University, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country or international organization, unless required to do so by applicable law (in which case ReCater will inform the University of that legal requirement before Processing, unless that law prohibits such information).
  • Ensures that all personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.
  • Implements the technical and organizational measures described in §7 to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.
  • Engages Sub-processors only in accordance with §5.
  • Assists the University in fulfilling its obligations to respond to Data Subject requests, in accordance with §9.
  • Returns or deletes Personal Data at the end of the provision of services, in accordance with §12.
  • Makes available to the University all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, in accordance with §10.

3. Subject Matter and Duration

3.1 Subject matter

The subject matter of the Processing is the operation of the ReCater platform on behalf of the University, as described in the Master Services Agreement and the Privacy Policy.

3.2 Duration

The Processing will continue for the duration of the Master Services Agreement between the University and ReCater. Upon termination, ReCater will return or delete the Personal Data in accordance with §12.

4. Nature and Purpose of the Processing

The nature and purpose of the Processing are:

  • To provide the ReCater mobile app, web dashboard, and related services to the University’s students, organizers, and dashboard admins.
  • To send push notifications about food posts and account events.
  • To generate monthly emissions summaries, optional STARS / Scope 3 / SB 253 reports, and per-university trend data.
  • To run points, badges, seasons, and raffles.
  • To moderate content and enforce the User Terms.
  • To respond to support requests.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

The Processing is not used for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose unrelated to operating the platform.

ReCater does not process the following:

  • Real names (unless configured for administrative display at the University’s election, in which case they are stored in the administrative layer only, not in the user-facing app or standard reports).
  • Student ID numbers, transcripts, course enrollment, financial aid records, health records, dietary records (other than the dietary flags entered by the Food Provider), or other education records.
  • Rosters imported from the University.
  • Biometric data.
  • Location data from the device.

6. Categories of Data Subjects

The Data Subjects whose Personal Data ReCater processes include:

  • Students of the University who have created accounts on ReCater.
  • Event organizers (typically student-organization officers or campus department staff) who post Surplus Food.
  • Dashboard administrators (approved by the University) who access the web dashboard.
  • Other authorized users of the ReCater platform, as determined by the University.

ReCater does not knowingly process Personal Data of children under 13. The Platform is designed for university affiliates who are at least 18 years old (or the age of majority in their jurisdiction).

7. Technical and Organizational Security Measures

ReCater implements the following technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, consistent with the requirements of GDPR Article 32 and industry best practices.

7.1 Technical measures

  • Encryption in transit: TLS 1.2+ for all client-server communication. HSTS is enabled.
  • Encryption at rest: encryption at the database and storage layer.
  • Row Level Security (RLS): PostgreSQL RLS policies enforce per-Tenant access at the database level. RLS applies to all tenant-scoped tables.
  • Role-based access control (RBAC): only approved dashboard admins can access Tenant data. Application-side checks verify role on every request.
  • Authentication: email-auth (Supabase Auth) or SSO (OIDC / SAML / InCommon). Session tokens are JWT-based with refresh tokens stored encrypted at rest.
  • Rate limiting: API endpoints are rate-limited to prevent abuse. Sensitive endpoints (exports, moderation) have stricter per-actor limits.
  • AI rate limiting: AI requests (OpenAI) are rate-limited per actor (5/day) with prompt sanitization and retry with exponential backoff.
  • Audit logging: security events (login, role change, export, moderation, breach detection) are logged to a tamper-evident audit log.
  • Sub-processor security: each sub-processor is required to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures.

7.2 Organizational measures

  • Personnel confidentiality: all ReCater personnel with access to Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations (in their employment agreements and ReCater’s information-security policy).
  • Access control: access to Personal Data is limited to personnel with a need-to-know.
  • Security training: ReCater personnel receive annual security and privacy training.
  • Vendor management: ReCater conducts security reviews of sub-processors before engagement and periodically thereafter.
  • Incident response: ReCater maintains documented incident-response procedures (PB-001 Security Breach Response, PB-002 Vulnerability Disclosure, PB-003 Data Loss Prevention).
  • Change management: changes to the platform that affect Personal Data processing are subject to code review, security review, and change-advisory board approval.
  • Backup and recovery: ReCater maintains daily backups with encryption, with documented restoration procedures.

7.3 Security certifications

ReCater is pursuing a SOC 2 Type I report as a future goal. Until a SOC 2 report is available, ReCater will provide the University with a written summary of its security controls (the “Security Summary”) on request. The Security Summary describes ReCater’s current control posture, including the measures listed in §7.1–7.2 and the sub-processor list in §8. The University may also conduct an audit under §10, or accept a third-party security assessment (e.g., a Vanta, Drata, or Tugboat Logic report) as evidence of ReCater’s security posture where one is available.

8.1 New sub-processors

ReCater will provide the University with at least 30 days’ notice before adding a new sub-processor that handles Tenant Personal Data. Material changes to existing sub-processor engagements will be communicated at least 15 days in advance. The University may object to a new sub-processor on reasonable grounds related to data protection; if the objection cannot be resolved, the University may terminate the affected services for convenience.

8.2 Sub-processor security

Each sub-processor is required to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, at least equivalent to the measures described in §7. ReCater periodically reviews sub-processor security posture; relevant security-review summaries are available on request to security@getrecater.com where disclosure is permitted.

9. Data Subject Requests

9.1 Controller handles

The University, as Controller, is responsible for responding to Data Subject requests (e.g., access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) under the GDPR or other applicable law. ReCater will not respond directly to Data Subject requests unless required by law or unless the University has authorized ReCater to do so in writing.

9.2 Processor’s assistance

ReCater will assist the University in fulfilling its obligations to respond to Data Subject requests. Specifically, ReCater will:

  • Provide tools (in the mobile app and dashboard) for Data Subjects to access, correct, or delete their own data.
  • Provide a data-export endpoint that the University can use to fulfill portability requests.
  • Target an initial response to the University’s request for assistance within 5 business days.
  • Implement technical changes (e.g., bulk data deletion) within 30 days of the University’s verified request.

9.3 Fees

ReCater’s assistance with Data Subject requests is included in the University’s subscription fee. If a Data Subject request is unreasonable, repetitive, or excessive, ReCater may charge a reasonable fee, with the University’s prior written approval.

10. Audit Rights

10.1 The University’s audit rights

The University may audit ReCater’s compliance with this DPA, including ReCater’s technical and organizational measures, once per calendar year, with at least 30 days’ written notice.

10.2 Audit methods

The University may conduct the audit by:

  • Reviewing ReCater’s written security and privacy documentation (including this DPA, the Privacy Policy, the security incident response procedures, and any available security certifications).
  • Requesting a security questionnaire or completed HECVAT from ReCater.
  • Requesting a video conference with ReCater’s security and privacy team to discuss controls.
  • Conducting an on-site audit at ReCater’s offices, with at least 60 days’ notice and at the University’s expense, no more than once per year.

10.3 Confidentiality of audit findings

Audit findings are the confidential information of the University and ReCater. The University may not disclose audit findings to any third party without ReCater’s prior written consent, except as required by law.

10.4 Substitute for audit

In lieu of an audit, the University may accept any of the following as evidence of ReCater’s compliance with this DPA: a SOC 2 report (Type I or Type II, when available), a third-party security assessment (e.g., Vanta, Drata, Tugboat Logic), or the Security Summary described in §7.3.

11. International Data Transfers

11.1 ReCater is US-based

ReCater is based in the United States. All Personal Data is hosted in the United States. The University acknowledges that Personal Data of Data Subjects located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom (UK), Switzerland, or other jurisdictions with data-transfer restrictions will be transferred to the United States.

11.2 EEA, UK, and Switzerland

For Data Subjects in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, ReCater relies on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Commission Decision 2021/914) for the transfer. The SCCs are available on request to legal@getrecater.com. ReCater will also cooperate with the University to provide additional safeguards (e.g., supplementary measures, encryption in transit and at rest, contractual controls) as required.

11.3 Other jurisdictions

For Data Subjects in other jurisdictions with data-transfer restrictions (e.g., Brazil’s LGPD, China’s PIPL, Canada’s PIPEDA), ReCater will work with the University to provide appropriate transfer safeguards on a case-by-case basis, which may include additional contractual clauses, supplementary measures, or local data residency arrangements.

12. Return or Deletion at End

12.1 Return

Within 30 days of the termination of the Master Services Agreement, the University may request a copy of all Tenant Personal Data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON). The export includes:

  • All posts and check-ins attributed to the Tenant.
  • All monthly emissions summaries and report history.
  • All survey responses.
  • All audit logs (subject to §10.3 confidentiality).

12.2 Deletion

Within 90 days of the termination, ReCater will delete all Tenant Personal Data from production systems and certify the deletion in writing. Audit logs are retained for 7 years for legal and compliance purposes, in a restricted-access archive that is not used for any operational purpose.

12.3 Sub-processors

ReCater will instruct all sub-processors to delete Tenant Personal Data within the same 90-day window, with written attestation of deletion.

13. Personal Data Breach

13.1 ReCater’s obligations

If ReCater becomes aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Tenant Personal Data, ReCater will:

  • Investigate and contain the incident within 48 hours of detection.
  • Notify the affected University Tenant within 72 hours of confirmation, with a description of the breach, the categories of Personal Data affected, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address it.
  • Cooperate with the University’s investigation and remediation.
  • Document the breach, the response, and the lessons learned in a written incident report, available to the University on request.

13.2 The University’s obligations

The University is responsible for:

  • Notifying affected Data Subjects in accordance with the GDPR, state breach notification laws, or other applicable law.
  • Notifying Supervisory Authorities in accordance with the GDPR (within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, where applicable).
  • Coordinating with ReCater on the content of any public statement or press release about the breach.

13.3 No liability for the University’s failures

ReCater is not liable for the University’s failure to notify Data Subjects or Supervisory Authorities, except to the extent that the failure is caused by ReCater’s own breach of this DPA.

14. Liability

14.1 Liability cap

Unless the Master Services Agreement states otherwise, ReCater’s total liability under this DPA is capped at the greater of (a) USD $100 or (b) the total amount paid by the University to ReCater in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim. Nothing in this DPA limits liability that cannot be limited under applicable law.

14.2 Data breach liability

Personal Data Breach liability is governed by the limitation of liability in the Master Services Agreement, if one exists. If no Master Services Agreement exists, the cap in §14.1 applies, except to the extent applicable law prohibits that limitation.

14.3 Indirect damages

Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, except for liability arising from a party’s gross negligence or willful misconduct.

15. Term and Termination

15.1 Term

This DPA is effective from the Effective Date and continues for the duration of the Master Services Agreement.

15.2 Termination

This DPA terminates automatically upon termination of the Master Services Agreement, except for those provisions that by their nature survive termination (including §10.3 Confidentiality of audit findings, §12 Return or Deletion at End, §13 Personal Data Breach, §14 Liability, and §16 General Provisions).

15.3 Effect of termination

Upon termination, ReCater will return or delete the Personal Data in accordance with §12. The University may, at its election, request an extension of the return-or-delete window for up to 90 days for legitimate operational reasons (e.g., transition to a successor platform).

16. General Provisions

16.1 Order of precedence

In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Master Services Agreement or the Privacy Policy, this DPA controls with respect to the Processing of Personal Data.

16.2 Severability

If any provision of this DPA is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in effect, and the unenforceable provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to be enforceable.

16.3 Amendment

ReCater may amend this DPA from time to time. Material amendments will be communicated to the University at least 30 days in advance. If the University does not agree to the amendment, the University may terminate the affected services for convenience.

16.4 Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the state of [STATE — to be specified in the executed agreement], without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

16.5 Dispute resolution

Disputes under this DPA are subject to the dispute-resolution provisions of the User Terms §11, except that Data Subject requests and Supervisory Authority inquiries are not subject to arbitration and may be addressed through the courts of the state specified in §16.4.

16.6 Entire agreement

This DPA, together with the Master Services Agreement, the User Terms, the Privacy Policy, the Food Safety Statement, and the University-specific Addenda, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the Processing of Personal Data.
