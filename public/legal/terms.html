1. Joining the ReCater Movement

1.1 Mission

ReCater is the verified impact loop for campus food recovery. We connect members of the campus community — student organizations, campus departments, and food providers — with each other so that surplus catered food from events and meetings reaches students who can claim it in time. ReCater is an information platform: we send notifications, generate audit records, and produce reports. We do not prepare, inspect, transport, store, or serve food. The platform exists because universities have asked for a more controlled, auditable way to share surplus food than email lists, group chats, or general-purpose sign-up tools allow.

1.2 The Good Samaritan framework does not apply

Members of the campus community — such as student organizations, campus departments, and food providers — are unable to donate food to nonprofit agencies under the federal Good Samaritan Food Donation Act (42 U.S.C. § 1791) because these organizations are not recognized as parties protected by the Act. ReCater’s role is to connect surplus food with students who can claim it on the campus where it was prepared. The “donation” framework does not apply to this workflow. The relevant framework is the assumption of risk the user accepts when consuming leftover food, as described in §3 (the “Same Risk as Event Guest” Principle) and the companion Food Safety Statement.

1.3 What ReCater does

  • Operates a branded mobile app for students to receive pickup alerts and check in to claims.
  • Operates a branded web dashboard for university sustainability offices and approved administrators to monitor activity, export sustainability reports, and moderate content.
  • Provides QR-code check-in at pickup, which records the time and the parties involved.
  • Generates monthly emissions summaries, optional STARS / Scope 3 / SB 253 reports (with the Dashboard Add-On), and per-university trend data.
  • Provides points, badges, and seasonal rewards that recognize repeat participation.
  • Runs raffles for prizes, where the university opts in.

1.4 What ReCater does not do

  • ReCater does not prepare, inspect, transport, store, or serve food.
  • ReCater does not independently verify that food available through the platform is safe for consumption, free of allergens, or otherwise suitable for any particular user.
  • ReCater does not provide insurance, indemnification, or any warranty as to the safety, quality, or suitability of food distributed through the platform.
  • ReCater does not make any decision on behalf of a university’s sustainability office about credit eligibility, scope boundary, or third-party assurance for sustainability reporting. ReCater provides structured evidence; the university makes the call.

1.5 Definitions

Throughout these Terms:

  • “User” means any individual who has created an account on ReCater, whether student, organizer, dashboard admin, or other authorized role.
  • “Food Provider” means a User who creates a food post on the platform. Food Providers are typically event organizers, student-organization officers, or campus department staff.
  • “Surplus Food” means food from a registered food business (such as catered meals) that remains after the main portion of an event is ending but is distributed before or during the cleanup period. Distribution must comply with the catering guidelines to ensure proper handling and safety.
  • “Pickup Window” means the time range during which a posted food item can be claimed. Pickup Windows are set by the Food Provider when creating a post.
  • “Event Guest” means any person who would have been present at the original event and consumed the food on-site had it not been shared.
  • “QR Check-In” means the act of a User scanning the venue QR code at the time of pickup, which records the pickup in the platform’s audit log.
  • “Pseudonym” means a generated name (e.g., “Bread Falcon”) assigned to a User at signup, used in place of the User’s real name throughout the platform.
  • “Tenant” means one university’s scoped ReCater environment, including users, posts, reports, settings, and branding.
  • “Authentication Layer” means the technical mechanism by which a User proves their identity to log in (email address in Supabase Auth, or opaque IdP identifier from university SSO).
  • “In-App Identity” means the pseudonymous name and avatar other Users see — never the real name, never the authentication-layer email.

1.6 Food Provider verification

To post food on ReCater, a User must be a verified, active member of an approved campus community. Verification is performed via:

  • A campus email address at an approved email domain registered for the University Tenant you are joining (the email-auth tier); OR
  • An OIDC / SAML assertion from a university Identity Provider that ReCater has been configured to trust (the SSO tier).

In either case, the University Tenant may impose additional restrictions on who is allowed to post (e.g., faculty and staff only, or specific student organizations). The Food Provider confirms they are authorized to post on behalf of the event’s host organization by checking a box at post creation. ReCater does not independently verify this authorization.

1.7 University-event-only scope

Surplus Food posts are only accepted for events held at or in association with the University Tenant. Posts for events outside the university community, or posts by Users not affiliated with the Tenant, may be removed by approved administrators without notice. ReCater is not a peer-to-peer food-sharing platform, a community fridge, or a food pantry locator; it is a campus-internal food-recovery system.

These Terms do not waive any legal protection that cannot be waived under applicable law. Nothing in these Terms is intended to override the rights of a User under the user’s university policies, the laws of the user’s state, or federal law (including FERPA, where applicable — see §10).

Eligibility to use ReCater is determined by your University Tenant. The university may set additional eligibility requirements (e.g., limiting to currently-enrolled students, or to specific staff roles). ReCater relies on the university to enforce these requirements.

  • You are responsible for the security of your account credentials.

3.4 In-app identity is pseudonymous

Your in-app identity is your Pseudonym, not your real name. Your Pseudonym is generated at signup and cannot be changed without administrative action. Other Users, including Food Providers and dashboard admins, see only your Pseudonym, not your real name or email.

3.5 Authentication: SSO and email-auth

ReCater supports two authentication paths, depending on your University Tenant’s configuration:

  • Email-auth (the default for new Tenants): you sign up with your campus email at the University Tenant’s approved email domain. The email is stored in ReCater’s authentication layer (Supabase Auth) for login and account recovery, and is never copied into the platform’s other tables, posts, or reports. The email is not displayed in the app or in standard reports.
  • SSO (available for Tenants that have configured a university Identity Provider): you sign in via the university’s existing OIDC / SAML flow. ReCater receives an opaque identifier from the IdP (and optionally a verified email claim used for account matching during migration). The opaque identifier is what ReCater stores; real name and email remain with the IdP. ReCater is designed to integrate with InCommon Federation member institutions.

Your University Tenant determines which path is available. Your authentication-layer email or IdP identifier is never displayed in the app, included in standard reports, or shared with other Users.

3.6 Notifications and Posts

  • A food post, once submitted and approved by the system, is final. Food Providers cannot edit a post after it has been published. Food Providers can end a post early (e.g., when the food is gone) by tapping “End Post” in the mobile app, which sends a closing notification to subscribers.
  • Push notifications are sent to opted-in Users within the relevant Tenant when a new post is published.
  • An “I’m on my way” informational button may be available; it is informational only and does not reserve the food. The first User to scan the venue QR code at the time of pickup is the one who claims the meal. No-shows after “I’m on my way” are tracked and may affect your account standing.

3.7 The “Same Risk as Event Guest” Principle

When you consume Surplus Food through ReCater, you assume the same risk as an Event Guest would have assumed had they consumed the same food on-site at the original event. ReCater does not transform, alter, or substitute the food; we route it from a posted event to a student who claims it within the posted Pickup Window. The food safety, allergen disclosure, and handling rules that applied at the original event apply unchanged. ReCater does not provide additional warranties.

3.8 Pickup Protocol

  • A pickup requires the User to scan the venue QR code (or otherwise check in) within the Pickup Window. The QR check-in records the time, the food post, and the User’s Pseudonym in the platform’s audit log.
  • Bring your own container. ReCater does not supply to-go containers, plates, or wrap.
  • Only enter the venue during the Pickup Window. Outside the Pickup Window, the venue is being cleaned or has been returned to its prior use.
  • Do not eat in the venue unless the venue has explicitly authorized eating on-site.
  • Respect the venue’s rules and the host’s instructions.

3.9 Cancellations

  • The Food Provider may end a post early. An “ended” notification is sent to Users who had tapped “I’m on my way.”
  • If the Food Provider ends the post before the Pickup Window opens, no claim has occurred and no audit entry is created.
  • ReCater reserves the right to cancel or pause any post at any time for moderation, safety, or operational reasons.

7. Points, Badges, and Rewards

7.1 Points

Users earn points for participating in food rescue. Points are calculated based on:

  • Compliance bonus: points for completing the post-event survey and providing accurate allergen and dietary information.
  • Food weight rescued: points proportional to the estimated weight of the food the User helped rescue.
  • Repeat check-ins: bonus points for Users who complete multiple QR check-ins within a season.

ReCater reserves the right to modify the points formula at any time, with notice. Points have no monetary value and are not redeemable for cash.

7.2 Badges

ReCater awards achievement badges to recognize participation milestones (e.g., “First Pickup,” “10 Pickups,” “First 100 Pickups,” “Streak” badges for consecutive-day participation). Badges are pseudonymous and visible only to the User and (in aggregate) to the University Tenant. Badges are awarded automatically based on activity and cannot be transferred.

7.3 Seasons

Each University Tenant runs its own seasons with defined start and end dates, aligned to the academic calendar. Points reset at the end of each season. Season progress is visible to the User and (in aggregate) to the University Tenant.

7.4 No employment relationship

Participation in points, badges, and seasons does not create an employment, contractor, or agency relationship between the User and ReCater or the University Tenant. Points and badges are recognition only and do not constitute compensation.

  • Be of the food being posted, taken at the event.
  • Be no larger than 10 MB.

  • Not contain text overlays, watermarks, or other third-party branding (other than the caterer’s own).

  • Push notifications: rate-limited by the platform’s notification service.

Repeated attempts to exceed rate limits may result in temporary account suspension.

ReCater uses a 5-report auto-hide threshold: when a post receives 5 reports from distinct Users, it is automatically hidden from the feed pending administrator review. Hidden posts are visible only to the Food Provider, approved administrators, and the moderation team.

14. Environmental Impact of Food Choices

14.1 Methodology

ReCater’s emissions estimates use emission factors derived from Poore & Nemecek (2018) and FAO publications, applied to estimated food weight categorized into the FG01–FG13 food group taxonomy. The classification itself is performed by an LLM (OpenAI), with the model version recorded for auditability. Methodology versions are tracked; historical reports can be reproduced with the same parameters.

14.2 Per-serving breakdown (max-of-range, kg CO₂e per serving)

  • Dairy: 0.6
  • Produce: 0.3
  • Grains: 0.5
  • Poultry, pork, seafood: 1.0
  • Beef, lamb: 4.0

14.3 Important disclaimer

These figures are directional sustainability indicators, not precise measurements. They are intended to support, not replace, the University’s own reporting decisions. The University remains responsible for the final accounting, scope boundary, credit eligibility, and third-party assurance decisions. ReCater does not warrant the accuracy of the estimates for any specific reporting purpose.

14.4 Methodology versioning

Every report records the emission factor version, the classification methodology version, and the LLM model version used. Reports can be reproduced with the same parameters to verify historical numbers. Methodology changes are versioned and documented; changes that materially affect the numbers will be communicated to University Tenants in advance.

A.1 Disclaimer of Liability

ReCater is an information platform. ReCater does not independently verify that food available through the platform is safe, allergen-free, or otherwise suitable for any particular user. Food Providers, Event Guests, and other Users assume equal risk when consuming Surplus Food through ReCater as they would assume when consuming the same food on-site at the original event. A.2 Responsibility of Event Organizers (Food Providers)

  • Follow your caterer’s food-safety guidelines at all times.
  • Reserve the venue for the Pickup Window and the cleanup period. Do not extend the Pickup Window beyond what was posted.
  • Comply with the Food Location and Event Continuity Policy: the food remains at the original serving location and is not relocated.
  • Approved food sources: university dining services, licensed caterers, campus retail, and club-purchased food from licensed vendors. Homemade, potluck, takeout, and unlicensed food are prohibited.
  • Schedule pickups to align with catering cleanup. Aim for 15-minute windows. Perishable food must be claimed within 4 hours of leaving temperature control.
  • Comply with your university’s policies on event registration, food service, and venue use.
  • Liability protection rests on the caterer (or, for homemade items, the food preparer). ReCater does not assume that liability.

A.3 Responsibility of Students Picking Up Food

  • You assume the same risk as an Event Guest when consuming Surplus Food through ReCater. This is the “Same Risk as Event Guest” Principle (see §3.7).
  • Only enter the venue during the posted Pickup Window. Outside that window, the venue is being cleaned or has been returned to its prior use.
  • Vacate the venue promptly after pickup. Do not remain in the venue after the Pickup Window closes.
  • Do not eat in the venue unless the venue has explicitly authorized eating on-site.
  • Bring your own container. ReCater does not supply to-go containers, plates, or wrap.

A.4 Pickup Window Guidelines

  • Pickups occur during the original event reservation or in the window immediately after the main portion of the event ends.
  • The 15-minute window is the default. Longer windows are configurable per Tenant.
  • The catering staff begins cleanup immediately after the Pickup Window closes. Pickups do not extend the event reservation.
  • Posts are final once published. The Pickup Window cannot be extended after the fact.

A.5 General Conduct

  • Respect the venue, the caterers, the event organizers, and other Users.
  • Do not consume alcohol or drugs in the venue.
  • Do not leave personal belongings behind.
  • Follow the venue’s safety guidelines (e.g., no open flames, no food on the floor, no consumption in restricted areas).
  • If the venue has a designated consumption area, use it.

A.6 Prohibited Activities and Safety Guidelines

  • Prohibited food sources: homemade, potluck, takeout, restaurant leftovers intended for personal use, unlicensed vendor food, food past its labeled date.
  • Prohibited platform content: see §9B.1 above (illegal content, hate speech, harassment, commercial advertising, impersonation, malicious code, privacy violations).
  • Prohibited pickup behavior: entering the venue outside the Pickup Window, eating on-site without authorization, taking food for distribution outside the platform, and any behavior that violates the venue’s rules.
